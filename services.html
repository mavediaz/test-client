At Bayview Rehabilitation we value your health and aim to provide you with the exceptional care you need and the care you deserve

Physiotherapy

Physiotherapy is a primary care, patient-focused health profession directly involved in movement sciences and aims to enhance or restore function of multiple body systems. Our team is committed to health, lifestyle and quality of life by incorporating a broad range of physical and physiological therapeutic interventions and aids.

Acupuncture

Acupuncture is a healing technique that has been studied and utilized for over 2500 years. Using ancient scientific principles, it is used to encourage natural healing, improving mood and energy, reduce and relieve pain and improve function of affected areas of the body. At Bayview Rehabilitation, our team utilizes the principles of acupuncture as either an adjunct to traditional physiotherapy or as a standalone modality for treating all musculoskeletal conditions.

Electroacupuncture

Similar to acupuncture, electroacupuncture functions to improve healing, reduce pain and improve function. This technique involving attaching a device that generates continuous electrical pulses using small clips allows several pairs of acupuncture needles to be stimulated simultaneously and at varying impulses. Electroacupuncture is used by our team to enhance your acupuncture experience.

Lumbar and/or Cervical Traction

Lumbar and/ or Cervical Traction involves stretching the spine with the goal of relieving back or leg pain caused by bulging, degenerating or herniated discs. Our team utilizes the both manual or mechanical traction in delivering relief.

Massage Therapy

Massage therapy involves hands-on manipulation of the soft tissues of the body, muscles, connective tissues, tendons and ligaments and joints for the purpose of optimizing health. It has a therapeutic effect on the body and optimizes health and well-being by acting on muscular, nervous and circulatory systems. Our massage therapists use a variety of techniques to improve function and relieve pain caused my dysfunction in these systems.

Prenatal Massage

Prenatal massage improves circulation, reduces stress hormones in your body, relieves insomnia and decreases the added stresses on muscles and joints associated from carrying a child including neck and back pain, leg cramping and sciatica. Our team can help to relieve carpal tunnel pain, swelling in hands and feet and alleviate headaches and sinus congestion – all of which are common pregnancy problems.

McKenzie Method – Mechanical Diagnosis & Therapy®

Bayview Rehabilitation is a Certified McKenzie Clinic. The McKenzie Method is a comprehensive approach to the spine that involves a systematic progression of applied mechanical forces which utilizes pain responses to monitor change in motion/function. Treatment (also known as MDT) involves reproducing the forces that have been identified in resolving the pain quickly in order to restore function and independence.

Please visit the McKenzie Institute’s website for more information: www.mckenziemdt.org.

Manual Therapy

Manual Therapy is a specialized area of physiotherapy that involves the management of neuromusculoskeletal conditions based on clinical reasoning, using highly specific treatment approaches including gentle, hands-on techniques combined with therapeutic exercise. Our team utilizes their manual therapy training to mobilize muscle tissue and stiff joints to help decrease pain and joint dysfunction.

Soft Tissue Release

This technique involving myofascial release, assistive stretching and massage includes applying precise pressure during a specific stretch performed across all planes of movement. It is effective in reorganizing scar tissue, returning muscles to proper resting length, correcting muscle imbalances, improving muscle function and decreasing pain.

Temporomandibular Joint (TMJ) Dysfunction

Being the most active joint in our body, the temporomandibular joint is often associated with painful jaw movement used in speech and mastication. With unique approaches to treating muscle disorders, internal disc derangements, subluxations and arthritic conditions associated with this joint, our team is dedicated to improving your ability to speak and chew.

In-Home Physiotherapy Services

In-home based physiotherapy provides assessment and treatment to our clients in their homes to improve overall physical well-being, enhance social functioning, increase independence and teach self-management. Our team will travel to your house, condominium or apartment within a 10 – 15 kilometer radius from our clinic to provide you with the physiotherapy care you require.

Motor Vehicle Accident Rehabilitation

A motor vehicle accident can be an extremely traumatic event. At Bayview Rehabilitation, our team is dedicated to working with patients suffering from whiplash, soft tissue injuries and functional limitations resulting from motor vehicle accidents. Additional services include in-home physiotherapy for those unable to leave their homes, job site assessments and completion of all insurance forms.

OHIP-Funded Physiotherapy Services

Bayview Rehabilitation is a publically funded physiotherapy clinic. To be eligible for these services, patients must be referred by a physician or nurse practitioner based on the findings of an assessment that the individual requires physiotherapy services, have a valid Ontario Health Card and fall within one of the following categories:

  • Aged 65 years and older
  • Aged 19 years and younger
  • Recently discharged as an inpatient of a hospital and in need of physiotherapy clinic services that are directly connected to the condition, illness or injury for which the person was admitted to the hospital; or
  • Patient is eligible for funding of services under the ODSP or OW program.

ADP Walker and Wheelchair Assessments

Our team is active registered Authorizers with the Ministry of Health and Long-Term Care for ambulation aids and wheelchairs. Our patients undergo a full function assessment including gait analysis, balance and strength testing in order to determine their optimal ambulation/seating needs. Education on the proper use of these devices will follow. To learn more about the ADP program please visit: health.gov.on.ca/en/public/programs/adp/.

Customized Orthotics

Customized orthotics are devices designed to align the foot and ankle into the most anatomically efficient position. They appear similar to insoles but utilize biomechanical appliances that are customized to correct specific foot imbalances. They function to reduce stress and strain on your body associated with poor foot mechanics when standing and/or walking commonly associated with foot, leg or back problems. At Bayview Rehabilitation, we can prescribe custom orthotics to help correct your specific foot and body imbalances.

Sports Rehabilitation

Whether our clients are high level athletes or a weekend warriors, Bayview Rehabilitation provides our clients with sport specific physiotherapy to ensure muscle or joint recovery is optimized with the training required to excel in their sport of choice.

Taping

Taping is used when large stressors are placed on muscles and joints causing pain and tiredness. This technique promotes healing and renews fatigued and/or injured muscles in order to allow those muscles and joints to sustain large forces over time. At Bayview Rehabilitation, taping techniques, in conjunction with manual therapy and exercises are used for treating sports injuries and various orthopedic and circulatory conditions and have been shown to optimize your recovery.

Electrical Modalities

Interferential Current Therapy (IFC), transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS), laser therapy and therapeutic ultrasound are noninvasive modalities used to promote healing, decrease muscle tension and increase circulation. Our team utilizes these devices as an adjunct to manual therapy and exercises to relax tissues and speed up the recovery process.

Pelvic Health Physiotherapy

Pelvic floor problems can be caused by weak or tight pelvic floor muscles. When muscles are too weak, they contribute to incontinence and organ prolapse. Tight muscles cause pelvic pain or fluctuations in the frequency of urination, constipation and chronic conditions. Pelvic health physiotherapists are specialty-trained in treating these conditions and are indicated as the first line of defense for a range of pelvic floor dysfunctions.
